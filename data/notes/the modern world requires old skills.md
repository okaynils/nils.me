Increasingly underrated skills to learn these days are reading well and explaining well.

The computer interface of the near future seems to be communication. Whether through text or speech, we will largely communicate with computers through language. Interfaces may narrow down to chat-like systems with fewer explicit controls. You will not need to press buttons as often if you can express your intent to an LLM precisely.

Reading is one of the best ways to learn precise language. It improves your vocabulary, shows you how to structure ideas, and teaches you how to express the same thought in different ways. That matters when you want a human or a machine to understand exactly what you mean.

But reading is only one half of the loop. A book gives ideas to you. It is your turn to give ideas to others. If language is becoming the interface, then explaining clearly becomes just as important as understanding deeply.
